Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment

Crypto Trading Analysis using Python & Data Visualization

Introduction

This project analyzes the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment and trader behavior using historical trading data from the Hyperliquid platform combined with the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index. The objective is to understand how market psychology influences trading performance, risk-taking behavior, and capital deployment in crypto markets.

By integrating sentiment indicators with execution-level trade data, the analysis uncovers behavioral patterns across different market regimes such as Fear, Greed, Extreme Fear, and Extreme Greed. The project demonstrates sentiment-aware analytics for trading intelligence and risk management.

Objective

• Analyze the impact of market sentiment on trader profitability and performance.

• Study changes in trade size, trading activity, and capital allocation across sentiment regimes.

• Measure risk behavior using PnL volatility and distribution metrics.

• Identify behavioral differences between Fear and Greed market conditions.

• Build an analytical dashboard to demonstrate applied data science skills in crypto trading.

Methodology

• Collected historical trade-level data from Hyperliquid and daily Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index data.
• Cleaned and standardized timestamps, numerical fields, and sentiment labels using Python (Pandas, NumPy).
• Merged datasets using a date-based left join to preserve all trade records.
• Calculated KPIs including average PnL, win rate, trade size, volatility, and trading volume.
• Visualized insights using Matplotlib for clear interpretation.

Results

The analysis shows that Greed and Extreme Greed regimes are associated with higher trade sizes, increased market participation, and better average profitability. Fear-based regimes exhibit higher PnL volatility and more defensive trading behavior.

Trade size distribution analysis reveals strong positive skewness and high kurtosis, indicating that a small number of extreme trading days dominate overall market activity.

Summary

This project demonstrates how market sentiment can be integrated with trade-level data to analyze trader behavior in cryptocurrency markets. The insights highlight sentiment-driven shifts in risk appetite, profitability, and market participation across different regimes.
